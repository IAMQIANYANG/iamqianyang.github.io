What is data visulization?

A simple definition of data visualization could be: "it is the study of how to represent data by using a visual or artistic approach rather than the traditional reporting method" (Yuk & Diamond,2014). More specifically, data visualization is the process of using visual representations to translate data into a visible form that highlights important features, including commonalities and anomalies (Thomas & Cook, 2005). It serves the goal of clear and effective communication of the intended information.

Successful data visualization involves both aesthetic design and functionality. Neither an ugly graph with great functionality nor an elaborately designed meaningless picture is an example of good visualization. As argued by Friedman (2008) “to convey ideas effectively, both aesthetic form and functionality need to go hand in hand, providing insights into a rather sparse and complex data set by communicating its key-aspects in a more intuitive way".

“Until recently, the term visualization meant constructing a visual image in the mind. It hase now come to mean something more like a graphical representation of data or concepts.Thus, from being an internal construct of the mind, a visualization has become an external artifact supporting decision making. Information visualization focused on the creation of approaches for conveying abstract information in intuitive ways."

Ware, 2013, p.2
